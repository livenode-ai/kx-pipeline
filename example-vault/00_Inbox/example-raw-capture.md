# Example Raw Capture

Recorded on the train home, roughly 6:20 p.m.

I keep thinking the remote work debate is framed around trust, but maybe the bigger thing is commute tax. Not just time. It is the mental setup cost before and after the actual office day.

Today I left at 7:40, arrived at 9:05, and by the time I had coffee and opened the laptop it was already 9:25. Then the first real thinking did not start until almost 10. On remote days, I usually get one clean 90-minute block before Slack gets noisy.

But it is not that the office is bad. The office is useful for messy alignment, onboarding, reading the mood, and solving ambiguous problems with people in the room. The problem is pretending every day needs that.

Possible rule: default remote for work that needs depth, planned office days for work that needs social bandwidth. Maybe the question is not "remote or office" but "what kind of cognitive state does this work require?"
